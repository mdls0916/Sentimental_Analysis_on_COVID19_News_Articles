Media Research - COVID-19 and Journalism

Coronavirus infections-19 (hereinafter referred to as coronavirus19) are spreading all over the world. Apps related to covid-019 have appeared, while some foreign media outlets have featured professional articles using data. Data journalism, which delivers fast and accurate information to readers by finding patterns and quantifying cases through data, is a hot topic. We should think about data journalism, which is closely related to changes in society.

Analysis Strategy

Using scraping (or web scraping) technology, data existing on the Internet was collected in an automated way. DATA JOURNALISM: Various media outlets are reporting on the covid-19.

The start of data journalism is closely related to the use of Computer Assisted Reporting (CAR) techniques called precision journalism. It uses sociological methodologies to collect, analyze and write information for news production to report close to scientific truth. In the process, with the help of analytical tools such as computers, we tried to maintain the rigor and precision of scientific research methodology. With this tradition maintained, the use of large-capacity data, called big data, became routine, and the contents of data journalism expanded to areas where data-based visualizations were used, storytelling techniques were presented with evidence through objectivity of data, and complex computations were used to capture invisible and unknown patterns. Of course digital journalism is in this background.

Trends in Data Journalism

According to trends so far, data journalism has been found to be in common with investigative journalism in many areas. The analysis work that finds hidden patterns through data, identifies structural factors, and shows them based on data fits well with investigative journalism. It also goes well with data operations that summarize fast-paced events, with emphasis placed on data summaries and presentation, not analysis. The task requires fast data processing and summary, or in-depth analytical skills, so people who do data journalism often require computer coding skills. A high level of coding ability is not essential for data journalism. Since this is an era in which reporters require not only coverage but also HTML or simple video editing skills, it is often believed that coding skills can be used to properly carry out data journalism. Therefore, journalists who carry out data journalism are required to have both the ability of engineers and the sense of journalists, not only the ability of numbers and computer coding, but also the ability of engineers to tell stories using data. Meanwhile, controversy arose over the argument that coding should be taught in journalism schools and that the content of teaching now is too much..
